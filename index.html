About Yogyakarta

Yogyakarta, a vibrant city in Indonesia, is renowned for its rich culinary heritage, captivating tourism spots, and deep-rooted traditions. Culinary delights like Gudeg, a sweet jackfruit stew, and Bakpia, a mung bean pastry, reflect the region's flavors. Tourists flock to iconic sites such as the Borobudur Temple and the Sultan's Palace, while traditional arts, including batik and gamelan music, showcase the city's cultural richness. Yogyakarta Tourist Destinations

for those of you who like to travel

TEMPLE
BOROBUDUR
Borobudur is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the largest Buddhist temple in the world, located in Central Java, Indonesia. Built in the 9th century, it features intricate carvings and 504 Buddha statues, making it a major pilgrimage and tourist destination.
TEMPLE
SEWU
Sewu Temple is an 8th-century Buddhist temple complex in Central Java, Indonesia, near Prambanan. It features a central stupa and 249 smaller shrines, known for its intricate carvings. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a popular tourist destination
TEMPLE
PRAMBANAN
Prambanan Temple is a 9th-century Hindu temple complex in Central Java, Indonesia. It is the largest Hindu temple in the country, dedicated to the god Shiva, and is famous for its intricate architecture and detailed reliefs.
CAVE
JOMBLANG
Jomblang Cave, near Yogyakarta, Indonesia, is a vertical cave famous for its 60-meter depth and unique ancient forest inside. It features stunning geological formations and is a popular destination for adventure tourism.
BEACH
PARANGTRITIS
Parangtritis Beach in Yogyakarta, Indonesia, is known for its black sand, cliffs, and stunning sunsets. It offers activities like ATV riding and is rich in local folklore.
KERATON
PALACE
Parangtritis Beach in Yogyakarta, Indonesia, is known for its black sand, cliffs, and stunning sunsets. It offers activities like ATV riding and is rich in local folklore.
MALIOBORO
STREET
Malioboro Street is a famous shopping and cultural hub in Yogyakarta, Indonesia. Known for its vibrant atmosphere, it features shops, street vendors, and local cuisine, making it a popular destination for tourists and locals alike.
HEHA
SKY VIEW
Heha Sky View is a popular tourist spot in Yogyakarta, offering stunning panoramic views, photo spots, and a restaurant. It's a great place to enjoy the scenery and relax.
BEACH
INDRAYATI
Indrayati Beach, located in Gunung Kidul, Yogyakarta, is known for its white sand and clear waters. It offers beautiful ocean views but is not suitable for swimming due to strong waves.
TEMPLE
RATU BOKO
Ratu Boko is an archaeological site near Prambanan, Yogyakarta, believed to be a royal residence or fortress. It features historical ruins and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.
JOGJA
MONUMENT
Tugu Jogja, or Tugu Pal Putih, is a historic monument in Yogyakarta, built in 1755. It symbolizes the unity between the people and their leaders and serves as a popular landmark and tourist attraction.
